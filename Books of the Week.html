Our Weekly Top Picks

Archer's Voice by Mia Sheridan

This book was amazing and inspiring from start to finish. Archer finds strength in his partner to move forward through life and reclaim all that he has lost at the hands of others. Mia Sheridan's best work to date, I'm a big fan - Kate

All the Light We Cannot See by Anthony Doerr

A historic tale set during World War II set between France and Germany. The story follows a younger French girl, left on her own during troubled times and a German boy on the other side of the war that is destined to destroy her. This book left my on the edge of my seat the entire time. I love history and leearning about it and this book was the perfect balance of historic teaching and fiction. - Emma

Tracy Beaker By Jacqueline Wilson

A forever favourite of mine. Tracy Beaker is a young girl in a carehome, although she is supposed to follow the rules of the house... she prefers to cause chaos. This book is a mix of comedy, childish humour and pure feel-good tales of Tracy's life in the care sytem. I've re-read this book at least 10 times now and it never gets old. - Grace

Spare by Prince Harry

A biography written by Prince Harry of England himself. This biography gives insights into the life of the Prince and the downsides of the crown. This book wa great, it felt raw and true throughout. I appreciated having a real peak into the life of a royal for once, finally knowing what happends behind closed doors. - Gillian

Twisted Love by Ana Huang

This was my first reading anything by Ana and I can now confirm I am a fan! This is a work of fiction littered with betray, lies and hurt. The love in this book is truly twisted but no spoilers, READ IT (I bet you'll enjoy it as much as I did the first and second time :) - Kinga

Verity By Colleen Hoover

A thrilling piece of literature that will have you bitting your nails and cursing the air the entitre time. This book will keep you on your toes as Sloan is compelled to venture deaper into relations she has no business in. This book will leave you with questions...alot of questions. Luckily for us the epilogue answers them all so no cliffhangers, yay - Amber

The Diary of Anne Frank by Anne Frank

The gripping diary of Anne Frank takes us through her life, allowing us to view the life of those hunted during World War Two. The diary follows Anne till the day of her death and the events throughout are truly shocking. The diary of Anne Frank helped to shape my view of that dreadful war. - Angelina

The Hunger Games: the Ballad of Songbirds and Snakes by Suzanne Collins

If you've watched the film and liked it you'll ove this even more! The prequel to the Hunger Games series is absolutely shocking, filled with plottwists that will surely give you whiplash. From the chaos of Snow's mind the cunningness of Lucy-Gray's actions, this book will keep you wanting more till the end.- Sin√©ad
